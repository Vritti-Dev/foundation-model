Why now

    
The age of the open Internet is ending.
The age of guarded AI has begun.

    

      In 2025, the United States published the AI Diffusion Framework. It sorts
      every country in the world into tiers and decides who gets frontier models
      and who does not. Recent restrictions have already cut access for whole
      regions to capable models from American labs, including those that power
      the products you and your friends use every day.
    

    

      India is not on the top tier. We were warned. From this point forward,
      foundation AI is a sovereign capability, not a global commodity.
    

  







  

    

      

        
The Internet

        
Open by design.

        
TCP/IP, HTML, HTTP, SMTP. The protocols that built the Web were
           published as drafts and adopted by everyone. Anyone could spin up a
           server. No country could meaningfully gate it.

        
That openness is why India produced Flipkart, Razorpay, Zerodha, and
           a million indie developers. We never needed permission. We built on
           a public substrate.

      

      

        
Foundation AI

        
Guarded like a strategic asset.

        
A frontier foundation model costs hundreds of millions of dollars in
           compute to train and is governed like nuclear technology. The weights
           are not free. The training data is not public. Access is licensed,
           rate-limited, and revocable.

        
AI is not the next Internet. It is the next national capability. No
           country that depends on a foreign provider for the substrate of
           thinking is independent.

How a lesson works

      
Read. Edit. Run. Get PASS.

    

    

      

        
1

        
Live code in your browser.

        
The browser modules boot a real Python kernel right in the page
           (Pyodide plus NumPy). You edit, run, and inspect outputs without an
           install. Works offline after the first load.

      

      

        
2

        
Every lesson has a checkpoint.

        
A grader checks the property that defines correctness for that
           lesson. A correct implementation prints PASS. A broken one
           prints FAIL with the failing property named. The grader does
           not lie.

      

      

        
3

        
Colab modules paste back a token.

        
The PyTorch lessons open in Google Colab with one click. When
           training finishes, the notebook prints a one-line submission token.
           Paste it here. That records your progress.
